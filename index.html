Graduate Capstone

Data Strategy · Python · Retail Analytics

Enabling the Shift in Retail Using Data: A Case Study of Amazon

Creative Component · M.S. Information Systems, Iowa State University · 2019

End-to-end study of Amazon's data-driven retail strategy — from business model analysis to a working recommendation engine built in Python on real Amazon product data.

  • Business & strategy analysis — Deconstructed Amazon's business model, virtuous cycle, and core value propositions (price, speed, selection); mapped seven pricing strategies and the fulfillment network behind them.
  • Customer data strategy — Analyzed how Amazon's in-house CRM drives customer acquisition, retention, and extension, connecting data collection to measurable outcomes.
  • Technical execution — Performed exploratory data analysis on a real Amazon toy-products dataset (manufacturers, categories, pricing) and built a recommendation engine in Python using four approaches: review ratings, review volume, manufacturer, and product category.
  • Algorithm evaluation — Studied collaborative filtering, content-based filtering, and Amazon's item-to-item collaborative filtering — the engine behind an estimated 35% of its sales.
  • Risk & market analysis — Assessed retail disruption through the Toys "R" Us bankruptcy and identified strategic risk factors facing market leaders.
The TPM arc: business problem → data analysis → technical build → strategic recommendation. Understanding the why, executing the how, and communicating impact.
